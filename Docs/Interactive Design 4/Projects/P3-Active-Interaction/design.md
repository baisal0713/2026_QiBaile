# P3 — Active Interaction
**Lectures:** L6–L7
**Status:** Not started

## Description
The participant shifts from catalyst to agent. They have intention now — they grab, throw, place, press. The world isn't reading your body anymore, it's waiting for your decisions. Physics becomes expressive material: weight, friction, momentum are design choices, not technical defaults. Threshold mappings dominate.

## Interaction Core
- **Input:** Grab, throw, push, drag, press, place, arrange
- **Variable relationships:** Primarily Threshold (grab triggers event, object placed on surface activates response), some Direct (drag weight, throw force)
- **Output:** Room responses to deliberate actions — light shifts, sound, object activation, spatial transformation

## Interaction Palette
- Pick up and place — object on pedestal triggers room response
- Throw — ballistic arc, impact, environmental reaction (speed/force matters)
- Push and drag — heavy objects with resistance, momentum, settling sound
- Activate — diegetic UI: levers, buttons, pressure plates, chains
- Arrange — multiple objects in relation produce combined effects (reuses proximity logic between objects)
